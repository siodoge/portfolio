UX Case Study: AI Content Labels

Eye‑tracking, think‑aloud protocols, and interviews

How Users Notice and Trust AI Labels

For a research study at JMU, my teammate and I explored how short‑form video users perceive and engage with warning labels on AI‑generated content using Tobii eye‑tracking, think-aloud sessions, and semi‑structured interviews.[file:2]

We found that labels are often missed unless they are high‑contrast and placed near captions or usernames, and that skepticism toward AI can reduce engagement even when labels are clear.[file:2]

  • Planned and ran lab sessions with 4 college‑aged participants
  • Configured Tobii eye‑tracking hardware and calibration flows
  • Analyzed gaze patterns, interview transcripts, and engagement behavior
  • Proposed design guidelines for more visible, intuitive AI labels
View Full Research PDF

Methods

Eye‑tracking, think‑aloud, semi‑structured interviews, participant recruiting, and synthesis.[file:2]

Key Insight

"Better" labels are those aligned with natural viewing patterns—not just accurate wording.[file:2]
